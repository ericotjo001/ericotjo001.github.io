1. New world of AI agents: from extreme to less extreme

Has universal agentic AI finally arrived? OpenClaw is undoubtedly a milestone, but we might be missing a few critical components before the world becomes truly driven by AI agents. How might such an agentic world look like?

1.1. Agentic war (<1% probability)

Suppose every nation’s leadership wake up one day to a realization: AI agents will be the key to power and influence.

It’s not unreasonable. Agents are probably going to be transcendental if nobody puts any restraint to AI development. They will be able to traverse the internet, learn something new and complete tasks requiring world knowledge.

Not bad, but as a side note, they might be influenced by bad actors or, as the experts warned, they may exfiltrate themselves and threaten the world, hasta la vista.

Governments, finally realizing the gravity of the situation, started putting up defensive structures, seriously push ahead the agenda for sovereign AI systems. Great Firewall sounds familiar, no?

Out of a sudden, world agentic traffic is a lot about cross-border negotiations and cybersecurity, and a little bit about everyday tomfoolery. For every such situation, though, there exists an Express VPN to, you know, do anything else but what the regulations were set up to forbid, and there exist people who happily play along.

So what? Oh boy, in this scenario, just buy tonnes of popcorns and watch. Or maybe invest in offensive cybersecurity companies which grow through military contracts. You might 127x your investment overnight. But why is this scenario unlikely? First, you need to imagine that most world leaders (1) are competent, wise, dignified and (2) have just the right amount of anxiety and pride in their nations. We don’t usually get past number 1, and number 2 is a difficult balancing act.

1.2. Fragmented world agentic internet (somewhat likely?)

What we see now: Y Combinator, VCs, funds and other entities are championing the startups they believed in. Some startups grow big enough only to be acquihired, some others scrambling to set up things around the latest new trend, like OpenClaw, some others slip into obscurity, and only a handful win.

Will this be sustainable? Maybe yes, until HSIT hits the fan, like when cheap AI APIs become so cheap, prevalent and yet powerful enough that large AI providers somehow need to change their business models or run out of business, or PE/VC liquidity crisis gets bad enough startups get choked out of resources.

So what? Structural change. Maybe the entity that will win is a record label instead of a VC. You know, like Sony Music Entertainment. Startups might not even be startups anymore; they are brands on royalty contract with one agentic record label (let’s call it ARL), and what used to be called startups will become “talents” paid for providing AI services, agentically, automatically, ironically.

Current VCs then become traditional; they have been gatekeeping and have somehow become a significant means of distribution. But its distribution is very manual, like, “hey guys, we put $20M for this startup, check it out.” That is of course ancient compared to ARL, the new entity we mentioned above.

1.3. Less fragmented but corporate-dominated agentic internet (likely…?)

This is the business-as-usual scenario. Google remains prevalent in most countries, OpenAI somehow got out of all the chaos as a profitable company and its expansion succeeded, or OpenAI collapses but we have enough AI companies to keep the AI dreams afloat anyway so it makes no difference, and everyone still codes with Anthropic’s models. What about data sovereignty? In this scenario, it’s still a good topic to debate about, and life goes on. China and North Korea are still doing their stuff, of course, and before you know it, Africa is using Chinese internet.

So what? Which Agentic AI companies to invest in? Not so fast, read on. Google bought Windsurf core team. Meta probably continues to FOMO and buys up whatever is there. Oh yes, OpenAI starts to do that too. Will there ever be the next Manus that becomes big enough to rival the big guys? Hey, Perplexity is still there. That’s enough said, and great investors might have seen some patterns here. Good job.

2. Tech-app stack view

2.1. OpenClaw is missing a face (breakthrough in 2026?)

We’re still installing OpenClaw through terminal, which is okay, easy to do, and it has grown fast enough that it can do quite many things already. There are manuals on the internet teaching you how to do it, or some other people will do it for you for a sum.

But my gut feeling is, people don’t get comfortable enough with that. There might come a point where an interface – like Google Chrome – is *nice* enough for people to interact with their personal agent. Let’s call this AgentFace.

So what? Read on.

2.2. Winners sitting on the edge (breakthrough by 2027?)

But personal agent is now not “stable”, in a sense. You can have Claude or Gemini or ChatGPT as the brain of your agent, but how many people will truly pay for them like they’re paying another electricity bill? Not many? Many? Depends. Edge devices might get so good they could locally accommodate smaller but already extremely powerful models; we’re already seeing something like, say, Gigabyte’s AI TOP. Now with Gemma 4, TurboQuant and stuff like that, we’re all getting more edgy. Not funny.

Edge computation might get so efficiently good that we don’t really need to pay AI “electricity bill” anymore. This doesn’t mean Anthropic and OpenAI run out of business; they might just become the premium supplier.

So what? Every household will have an Agentic AI personal assistant by 2027? Maybe. But there’s one missing component. Read on.

2.3. The grand agentic internet layer (by 2027???)

Yes, the missing component might be a dedicated grand agentic internet layer. This is an abstract layer, and not entirely new, just not yet well-formed. it is already populated with things running across MCP servers, and, more recently, OpenClaw skills etc.

What’s missing is the thing that connects developers to the grand layer. Suppose in the near future people are comfortable with AgentFace that we mentioned earlier, developers should already be comfortable with coding assistant connecting whatever lives in AgentFace to the world. This is enabled by the invisible abstract grand layer that is to be shaped right now. And then we will have *nice* agentic apps dominating the world, built on AgentFace extension.

So what? There you go, agentic web. Invest in edge AI and the AgentFace company-to-be, then support agentic app developers. Maybe also find people crazy enough to want to build agentic record label (ARL) that I previously mentioned, because ARL will be the major brokers in this grand layer. Unfortunately, AgentFace variants might be locked under the device’s proprietary development, like Apple Intelligence, and fragmented tech remains the norm.

2.4. Big tech playbook

Why would big tech let all these play out independently? When Meta saw Manus, they bought it. When Sam Altman saw OpenClaw, he hired the guy away. If I were these big tech CEOs, I will be worried about competitors too.
